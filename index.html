Reproducitve Rights

Topic Background

The social issue that this project is centered around is reproductive rights. More specifically, we wanted to focus on abortion rights for women. The overturning of the Supreme Court case Roe v. Wade has affected many women across the United States, especially women of color or low-income women who live in states that have now banned abortion. Thus, we hope to use a motorized storyboard to depict the negative results of banning abortions.

Social Impact, Audience, and 5 Whys

Our main focus for our project was to convey how the overturning of Roe versus Wade could affect minority women, especially women who are of low income. It would affect our attended audience because it strongly utilizes pathos, which in turn, will cause our audience to take a stand for the purpose of our project. Our main character is a low-income woman called Anne who is pregnant and unable to support her child. As a result, she decides to abort her baby. But, she lives in Texas where Roe v Wade has been overturned so she has no choice but to turn to a legal abortion clinic. in this situation and is portrayed as a woman who has no other option than to seek something illegal that could eventually harm her well-being. With no choice, she bags a nurse at a nearby doctor's office to give her an abortion but she is curtly refused. This causes empathy for the audience when they see such a scene because she is a helpless person. Then the evil doctor which is the person that performs the illegal abortion on Anne is depicted as evil looking to invoke this kind of fear and concern for what would happen to and if she does carry out the illegal abortion. In the end, the dramatic and glaring red lights that shine between Anne interacting with the doctor create suspense that eventually leads to a red line (representing death)that passed away from the illegal abortion death rapping around Anne's neck indicating that she passed away from the abortion. We decided to give our story a sad ending mainly to emphasize that this isn't just a story but a reality that many low-income women face especially with the overturning of Roe v Wade. we hope that the dramatization of our project will lead our audience to understand why it is so crucial to give abortion rights to women because it is a part of their Reproductive Rights and something that is fundamental to women.
